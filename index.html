Billie Eilish

Billie Eilish is a genre-defying American singer-songwriter who rose to fame as a teenager with her 2017 debut EP, Don’t Smile at Me, and her chart-topping 2019 album, When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go?. Known for her signature "whisper" vocals and a dark, experimental pop sound crafted alongside her brother and collaborator, Finneas, she has become one of the most decorated artists of her generation. Eilish made history as the youngest person to win all four major Grammy categories in a single year and has since secured two Academy Awards for her cinematic ballads. Beyond her music, she is a powerful cultural icon recognized for her advocacy regarding mental health, body image, and climate change, continuously evolving her style and sound through acclaimed projects like Happier Than Ever and her most recent global success, Hit Me Hard and Soft.
